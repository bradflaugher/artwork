# unfollow

A triptych that turns the addictive social-media interface into fine-art refusal.
Three traps made visible. Each panel is beautiful enough to stop the scroll,
then unsettling enough to make you want to close the app.

Read the [manifesto](manifesto.md) first — the words are part of the piece.

---

## The triptych

Read left to right: the trap, the alarm, the emptiness after.

### I. the infinite scroll

<img src="the-infinite-scroll.png" alt="A phone-shaped panel in dawn-to-midnight blue. A feed of post-cards falls through it and out the bottom edge, the cards growing smaller and more transparent as they recede. A single notification-red dot marks a distant card far down, where the eye is trained to chase it." width="360" align="top"/>

> *There is no bottom. There was never meant to be a bottom. The bottom
> would let you leave.*

A feed that falls forever — vertigo where the product sells speed. The
notification-red accent appears once: a single dot on one distant card, far
down, where the eye is trained to chase it.

### II. the notification abyss

<img src="notification-abyss.png" alt="A dark teal panel, deep as water at dusk. The familiar red notification badge swells as it descends — 9, 99, 999 — each heavier, until the numbers lose meaning and the badge becomes a falling glowing weight sinking into the dark. Beneath it the silhouette of a head tilts toward the light of the badge." width="360" align="top"/>

> *It arrives as concern. It performs as need. It is a server, keeping you
> here.*

A red badge swelling into deep water — drowning where the product sells
rescue. The red is the only color that reaches the bottom. It reaches the
bottom because it is meant to pull you down with it.

### III. the ghost engagement

<img src="ghost-engagement.png" alt="A near-white panel, the color of a screen at 3 a.m. A single large engagement counter at center — a heart, the number 247, a thin underline. Around it, faint ghosted echoes of the same counter drift and fade. A single dying red pixel marks the heart." width="360" align="top"/>

> *You were here. The number says so. The number does not remember you.*

A like-counter draining to zero — haunting where the product sells
belonging. The number is frozen at a modest, very human `247`. No faces, no
names — just the residue of attention, already cooling.

## On the medium

These are not photographs of an interface. They are the interface, redrawn
by hand in its own language and held still. Each piece is drawn directly in
[SVG](the-infinite-scroll.svg) — vector code, committed as text — so the
medium is the same machinery the pieces accuse. SVG is text; text is
inspectable; inspectable is the opposite of the black box the feed prefers.

Open any `.svg` file and read exactly how the trick was drawn. That openness
is itself a small refusal of the way these products usually work.
