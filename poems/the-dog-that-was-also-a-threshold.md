# The Dog That Was Also a Threshold

The dog lay across the doorway
and the doorway took this personally.

To leave the room you had to step over him,
which the room recorded as an exit,
which the dog recorded as a betrayal,
which the hallway recorded as a draft.

He was not a large dog.
He had been a smaller dog, once, and a puppy before that,
and before that a notion the kitchen kept chasing.
Now he was a line drawn across the floor
that the whole house agreed not to cross after sundown.

Visitors asked, May we go.
The dog did not answer, because thresholds do not answer.
They only permit, or do not.
Tonight the dog did not.

We learned to live in the room we were in.
We learned to call it choosing.
The dog breathed in the grammar of the gap —
neither inside nor outside,
but the joint where the two keep failing to meet.

When he finally rose to drink,
the whole house exhaled,
then could not remember which side it was on.

> Gloss: *A dog is a door that has decided to lie down across its own purpose.*
