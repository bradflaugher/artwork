# Inventory of a Pocket

1. One key, to a door I have not owned since 2019.
2. One receipt, faded to a confession of ink.
3. One coin, foreign, denomination unknown, spent once in a dream.
4. One thread, the same color as a coat I gave away.
5. One ticket stub, *admit one*, the one never specified.
6. One folded apology, written on a napkin, mostly grease now.
7. One pebble, kept for a reason that has since emigrated.
8. One match, struck once, refused to light, kept out of spite.
9. One list, identical to this one, but older, and wrong.
10. One pocket, which holds all of the above and is, itself, held.

> Epigraph: *A pocket is a small museum curated by a thief who has forgotten the crime.*
