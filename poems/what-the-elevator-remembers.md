# What the Elevator Remembers

The elevator remembers the third floor
the way an old man remembers a wife:
specifically, and with the wrong details.

It remembers a man in a green coat
who pressed 3 and meant 7.
It has spent six years confusing them.
It opens on 3 now out of loyalty
to a mistake it has grown fond of.

It remembers a child who rode it
to the basement and back, forty times,
calling it a horse. The elevator
did not correct the child.
The elevator is not sure it isn't a horse.

It does not remember the lobby.
The lobby remembers the elevator
and resents it. This is the nature
of ground floors: they believe
they are the real ones, and that everything
above is a rumor the cables started.

> Epigraph: *A lift is a vertical animal trained on the number line.*
