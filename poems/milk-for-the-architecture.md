# Milk for the Architecture

The tenements were hungry
and we had brought milk.
The milk did not know
it was for buildings.
The buildings did not know
they were thirsty. We poured
anyway. It is important
to pour.

The stairwells drank first,
then the lintels, then the cornices
which had been ornamental
and were now lactating softly
onto the sidewalk below.

A banker came out and asked
what we were feeding the city.
We said: milk. He said:
the city is not an animal.
We said: the city is whatever
we pour for. The cornice wept
a little more, in agreement,
and a window opened
to ask for seconds.

> Epigraph: *A building that has been fed is a building that owes you.*
