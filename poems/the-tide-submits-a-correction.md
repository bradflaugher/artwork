# The Tide Submits a Correction

The tide tables were wrong.
The ocean sent a memo
in a bottle, which is how the ocean
sends all its paperwork
when it wants plausible deniability.

We read it at low water.
It said: *I have decided
to arrive earlier than scheduled.
The moon, I remind you,
is advisory only.
Its recommendations are noted
and filed in a trench
where light does not reach.*

The harbormaster filed a counter-memo
citing every tide table since 1847.
The tide did not respond.
The tide is a bureaucracy of one.
It does not hold meetings.
It does not take minutes.
It takes shorelines, mostly,
and redistributes them
to no one's satisfaction.

At the appointed hour
the water did not rise.
It waited. The boats leaned
like penitents. The pier groaned
like a creditor who knows
the payment will be late.

When the correction finally came
it was a single wave
that had been back-dated
and was therefore inadmissible.
The sea shrugged. The sea
is never wrong. The sea
has never once read the manual.

> Epigraph: *A tide is a schedule the ocean keeps meaning to cancel.*
