# Villanelle for a Stuck Turnstile

The turnstile will not turn, and will not let me through.
It is a wheel that has remembered how to grieve.
I push, and it pushes the same push back at you.

The commuters behind me are also stuck, and also two —
each one a body leaning at a body that won't leave.
The turnstile will not turn, and will not let me through.

I have a ticket. The ticket is correct and true.
The machine has read it. The machine does not believe.
I push, and it pushes the same push back at you.

A guard arrives and asks me what I'm trying to do.
I say: I'm trying to arrive. He says: arrive.
The turnstile will not turn, and will not let me through.

There is a city on the other side, in view,
where everyone has entered and no longer needs to heave.
I push, and it pushes the same push back at you.

I will be here at closing, and at opening, too,
still leaning, still submitted, still too polite to leave.
The turnstile will not turn, and will not let me through.
I push, and it pushes the same push back at you.

> Epigraph: *A villanelle is a turnstile: it turns, and turns, and turns, and will not let you through.*
