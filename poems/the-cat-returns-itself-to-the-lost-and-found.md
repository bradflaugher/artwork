# The Cat Returns Itself to the Lost-and-Found

The cat was never missing.
It had only been on loan
to the part of the house
that does not believe in cats —
the part where shadows
forget whose they are
and begin answering
to other people's names.

It walks back into the kitchen
the way a tenant walks back
into a flat that was rented,
briefly,
to the wrong silence.

We had filed it
under *domestic, small,*
asleep on a radiator
that is also a radiator
in a second room
we have never entered.
The filing was premature.
The cat files a correction.

It sits on the counter
and refuses to be a pet.
For the hour
between three and four
it is a piece of weather
that has wandered indoors
and does not know
how to be asked to leave.

We apologize to the cat
for the apology,
which it accepts
by knocking over
the one glass
we had not yet
learned to live without.

> Gloss: *A cat is a piece of weather that files a correction against your assumptions.*
