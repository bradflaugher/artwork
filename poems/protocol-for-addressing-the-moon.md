# Protocol for Addressing the Moon

```
PROTOCOL MOON-ADDR-2026
Status: ACTIVE
Classification: bedtime // not confidential // the moon is not listening anyway
```

**1. Determine whether the moon is present.**
The moon is frequently absent in the sense that matters (visible) and present in the sense that does not (gravitational). Address the moon as though it is present. The moon will not correct you. The moon has never corrected anyone.

**2. Select a mode of address.**
Permitted: *Moon*, *old light*, *the same one*, *you up there*, *witness*.
Forbidden: *lunar body*, *Earth's satellite*, *the moon unit*. These are the terms of people who have not earned the right to speak to it, which is everyone, but some of us try anyway.

**3. Do not ask the moon for anything.**
The moon has nothing to give that it has not already given, which is: light it stole from someone else, and a pull on the water that we mistake for a feeling. Asking the moon for more is a protocol violation and also a category error.

**4. If you must speak, speak in the second person.**
The moon responds best to *you*, which is also the pronoun of people who are not there, and the moon is a person who is not there, very reliably, every morning.

**5. Keep it short.**
The moon has been addressed for approximately 300,000 years. It is tired. If your address cannot be delivered in fewer than two (2) breaths, the moon is permitted to set before you finish.

**6. Closing formula.**
The recommended closing is silence, held for a count of three, followed by the word *anyway*, said to no one in particular. *Anyway* is the correct termination because the moon does not answer, and *anyway* is what we say to a conversation that has ended without us.

```
EXAMPLE TRANSMISSION:
  "Moon. You up there. Same one as my mother saw. Anyway."
END OF TRANSMISSION.
```

**7. Acknowledgement.**
No acknowledgement will be received. This is the protocol. The protocol is working as intended.

> Epigraph: *A protocol is a way of speaking to something that has agreed, in advance, not to reply.*
