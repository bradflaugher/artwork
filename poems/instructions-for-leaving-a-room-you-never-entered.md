# Instructions for Leaving a Room You Never Entered

**Step 1.** Locate the room. Since you did not enter it, it is not behind you. Look instead for a door you have been avoiding for unrelated reasons. The room is probably behind that.

**Step 2.** Do not open the door. You are leaving, not entering. Opening the door is an entering action and will invalidate the procedure.

**Step 3.** Stand with your back to the door. This is the correct orientation for leaving. If you feel the door behind you, you are in position. If you feel nothing, you are either already outside the room (in which case, skip to Step 7) or the door was a metaphor (in which case, skip to Step 9).

**Step 4.** Recall the time you spent in the room. You spent no time in the room. This is the ideal amount of time to have spent. Note it for your records: *time in room: none. room: not entered. feelings: several, all unexplained.*

**Step 5.** Say goodbye to someone who is not there. This is the most important step. The room is empty because you never entered it, but the goodbye is still owed, and debts do not require a room to be paid in.

**Step 6.** Walk away from the door. Do not turn around. Turning around is the first motion of entering, and you have already not entered this room once today; you do not need to not enter it twice.

**Step 7.** You are now outside the room. You have always been outside the room. Congratulations. The procedure was a formality.

**Step 8.** (Optional.) Forget the room entirely. A room you never entered is easiest to leave if you also never remember it. This step is optional because forgetting is not something you can be instructed to do, only something that happens to you, like weather, like Tuesdays.

**Step 9.** If the door was a metaphor: stop. You have already left. You left when you started reading.

> Epigraph: *The hardest room to leave is the one you never entered, because you can never be sure you're gone.*
