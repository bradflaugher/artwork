# The Rain Reverses

The rain began to fall upward
and the gutters, embarrassed,
spat the water back at the sky.

A child held out a cup
and caught nothing, then everything,
then the memory of a Tuesday
that had been cancelled for weather.

The puddles stood up and walked home.
The puddles had homes; we had not known.
They left small wet apologies
on the sidewalk, shaped like our shoes.

We stood in the reversed rain
and felt it leave us. It is a particular grief
to be rained on from the inside out,
to watch the weather undress you
and climb back into its cloud
like a lie that has finished its shift.

> Epigraph: *Some storms are not coming. Some storms are leaving.*
