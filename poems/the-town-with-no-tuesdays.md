# The Town With No Tuesdays

In the town with no Tuesdays
the week skips like a record.
Mondays fall directly into Wednesdays,
and Wednesdays arrive already exhausted,
having lived a day they cannot name.

The townspeople have a word for the gap:
they do not say it. To say it
would be to admit the week has teeth
and that some days are bitten out.
They leave Monday dinners on the table
and find them again on Wednesday,
colder than they should be
by only one night.

A visitor once asked what happened
to Tuesday. The mayor replied:
Tuesday is not a place. Tuesday
is what we call the silence
between the meal and the meal.
The visitor wrote this down
and the town took the pen
and ate it, gently, the way
a town eats a thing it does not
want repeated.

> Epigraph: *A calendar is a list of holes dressed as a list of days.*
