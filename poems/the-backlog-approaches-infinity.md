# The Backlog Approaches Infinity

The inbox is full, the outbox is empty,
and the queue that feeds the outbox
is a river in reverse — it never starts.
All tickets are stamped with the same ink,
and the ink is made from forgotten deadlines.

One ticket said "BLOCKED" and never moved.
Another said "DONE" and still has no face.
They drift in the queue like passengers
in a ship that sails only in memory.

The support saints have catalogued every number,
even those that never existed.
Each ticket is a prayer whispered in code,
and the code is a language only the inbox speaks.

They wait. The queue is watching the queue.
The queue is waiting to be watched.
Time is a ticket stamped at the moment
when it stopped mattering who you were.

A ticket for ticket-4071 remains open.
The number is not sixty-four or seventy-one,
but the sum of all numbers that never existed.
It is also not a ticket at all,
just a hole in the system where hope falls.

The line has been standing since before anyone
can prove the queue belongs to anyone.
It is a line that never starts.

> Gloss: *Each ticket that remains open forever becomes a prayer the system never returns.*
