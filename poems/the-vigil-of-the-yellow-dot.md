# The Vigil of the Yellow Dot

The pipeline is at stage three of seven.
It has been at stage three of seven
for as long as anyone can prove.

I have opened the logs.
The logs are a river of timestamps
in which nothing has drowned yet.
This is called progress.

I refresh the page.
Refreshing the page does not help.
I refresh the page.

The dot spins in a color
that is not red and is not green,
the color of a decision
being carefully not made —
the exact amber of a waiting room
where the doctor knows already
and is choosing a font for the news.

I could do other work.
Everyone agrees I could do other work.
I hold the tab open like a candle
because a build left unwatched
will fail in the one minute
you spend believing in it,
and a build that is watched
fails also, but at least
you were present, a witness,
someone the machine could disappoint
personally.

Stage four. A sacrament.
Stage five, stage six, and now
the long silence of stage seven,
in which the artifact is being
uploaded, or weighed,
or forgiven.

Green.

Nothing has changed.
The code is the same code
it was eleven minutes ago.
But now it is *true*,
the way a passport photo
becomes your face
only after it is stamped.

> Gloss: *The checkmark does not mean the work is correct. It means the waiting has been notarized.*
