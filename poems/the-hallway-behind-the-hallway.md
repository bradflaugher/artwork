# The Hallway Behind the Hallway

I opened the hallway and behind it
was another hallway, longer,
with the same wallpaper but quieter,
the way a photograph of rain
is quieter than rain.

I walked it. The doors were numbered
in a language I had forgotten on purpose.
Door 7 had a keyhole shaped like a question
someone had already stopped asking.
Door 8 was the same keyhole, older.

At the end of the second hallway
was a third hallway, painted
the color of the word *almost*.
I did not open it. I have learned
that hallways breed,
and that the hallway behind the hallway
is the one that remembers your shoes.

> Epigraph: *Every room I've left has a second room I didn't.*
