# The Trees Grew Downward Into Last Year

The arborist came with a ladder
and found the roots waving hello
from the attic. The roots had opinions
about the ceiling, which they called soil,
and the attic, embarrassed,
had begun photosynthesizing in secret.

We asked the trees what year it was.
The trees said: last year. Still last year.
The leaves had not yet arrived
because they were waiting below us
for permission to un-fall.

A squirrel filed a complaint
with the downstairs branch office.
The complaint was written in acorns
and translated poorly into bark.
It said: someone is living in my future.

We brought coffee to the stump.
The stump accepted it as rent.
It is the nature of trees
to collect what you owe them slowly,
in rings, without sending an invoice
until you have forgotten the debt.

> Epigraph: *A root is just a branch that decided gravity was negotiable.*
