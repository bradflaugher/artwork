# Mistranslation Cascade, No. 4

> Original (English): *The cat is on the mat.*

**Pass 1 — machine-translated to French, back to English:**
*The cat is on the carpet.*

**Pass 2 — to German, back to English:**
*The cat sits upon the matting.*

**Pass 3 — to Japanese, back to English:**
*As for the cat, it is on top of the rug.*

**Pass 4 — to Russian, back to English:**
*The tomcat is located on a small floor covering.*

**Pass 5 — to Korean, back to English:**
*The male cat is placed on the floor cloth.*

**Pass 6 — to Arabic, back to English:**
*The father-cat rests over a humble ground-fabric.*

**Pass 7 — to Hindi, back to English:**
*The grandfather of cats dwells above a woven humbleness.*

**Pass 8 — to Turkish, back to English:**
*The ancient cat ancestor inhabits the upper side of a textile humility.*

**Pass 9 — to Swedish, back to English:**
*The original cat-being lives on the textile that is itself a kind of modesty.*

**Pass 10 — to English (final, untranslated, kept):**
*The first cat lives on a modesty woven to be walked on, and has always lived there, and was never not on it, and the mat was always a confession, and the cat was always the one being confessed to, and the translation was never the point; the point was the soft thing under the soft thing, which we keep standing on, which we keep.*

> Epigraph: *A mistranslation is a sentence becoming more itself by becoming less exact.*
