# The Census of Snow

The snow arrives the way an audit arrives:
quietly, overnight, with no return address,
and by morning every roof has been counted
and assigned a number it will never be told.

It tallies the parked cars as households.
It files the dog as a small, warm building.
It records the bench in the park three times,
once for the bench and twice for the men
who were not there but should have been.

The snow does not keep what it counts.
This is the cruelty of the work.
By noon the figures run into the gutters,
the census dissolves into the census of water,
and the town stands recounted into nothing,
holding receipts that say only: *present, present, present.*

We are, each winter, briefly inventoried.
We are, each winter, briefly believed in
by something that cannot read its own ledger
and would not recognize us if it could.

> Epigraph: *To be tallied is not to be remembered. The snow proves this annually.*
