# Schedule C — Attachment of Regrets

```
Exhibit to Form 7B (Notice of Feeling)
Schedule C: Attachment of Regrets
Attach one regret per line. Do not staple the regrets.
```

**Line 1 — Regret, principal:**
The regret that I did not say *stay* when I meant *stay*, and instead said *fine*, which is the cheapest word in the language and the one I use to mean *I am leaving and I want you to notice*.

**Line 2 — Regret, contingent:**
The regret that I learned the word *fine* from my father, who learned it from his, who learned it from a man whose name we have lost and whose regrets are therefore unfiled and still circulating.

**Line 3 — Regret, deferred (carried from a prior tax year of the heart):**
The regret that I returned the dog. The dog was not a dog. The dog was the last time I was willing to be responsible for something that depended on me completely. I have not itemized this regret because it does not itemize; it is a lump sum and it is deductible only from joy.

**Line 4 — Regret, minor, recurring:**
The regret that I wave at people who are not waving at me, and then convert the wave into a hair adjustment, and then resent my hair.

**Line 5 — Regret, attachment to this attachment:**
The regret that I have filed my regrets at all, which is a way of keeping them, which is a way of saying they are mine, which is a way of saying I am not done with them, which is the only honest thing in this schedule.

```
Total regrets attached: 5
Total regrets unattached: unknown and unattachable
Signature of filer: ____(the same one, still filing)____
```

> Epigraph: *A regret you can attach is a regret you can also, in theory, detach. None of these are those.*
