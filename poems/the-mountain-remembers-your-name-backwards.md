# The Mountain Remembers Your Name Backwards

You climb and the mountain learns you
the way a door learns a hand:
by pressure, and wrong.

At four thousand feet it begins
calling you by a name you almost had
in a childhood you did not finish.
The name is backwards in the air.
You answer anyway. Politeness
is how climbers survive.

The summit files your footprints
under someone else's ascent.
It keeps a ledger of altitudes
measured from the inside out.
Your breathing becomes paperwork
the mountain stamps APPROVED
and files in a cloud shaped like regret.

You reach the top and find
you have been climbing down
the entire time. The mountain knew.
It waited at the bottom
with your name reversed,
waiting to be called correctly
so it could forget you again.

> Epigraph: *Altitude is a memory the ground keeps incorrectly.*
