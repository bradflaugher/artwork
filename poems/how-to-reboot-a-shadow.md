# How to Reboot a Shadow

## Before you begin

Confirm that the shadow is malfunctioning. A functional shadow will:
- Follow the body with a latency of approximately 0.05 seconds
- Darken the surface it falls upon proportionally to the occlusion of light
- Cease to exist when the light source is removed

If the shadow does none of these things, or does all of them in the wrong order, or refuses to match the body that cast it, or matches a body you do not recognize, proceed.

## Step 1 — Isolate the shadow from its light source

The shadow cannot be rebooted while attached to a light source it depends on. Move the body into total darkness. Wait 90 seconds. During this time the shadow may thrash, flicker, or attempt to attach to nearby objects. This is expected. Do not re-enter the light early. A shadow that is partially rebooted will lag two frames behind the body, which is worse than a shadow that lags one frame, which is already bad.

## Step 2 — Enter safe mode

In total darkness, announce the following incantation:

```
SHADOW / SAFE MODE / ACKNOWLEDGE
SHADOW / SAFE MODE / ACKNOWLEDGE
```

If the shadow replies (it will not reply — it has no mouth — but sometimes a shadow will dim itself further, which is its version of speech), it has entered safe mode. If the shadow does nothing, it was already in safe mode, or it has been running in safe mode since you were twelve, and the procedure is still valid.

**Safe mode symptoms:**
- The shadow renders as a silhouette only, no detail
- The shadow does not approximate hair, fingers, or the creases of clothing
- The shadow appears slightly earlier than the body that casts it

These symptoms are not bugs. They are the shadow's way of saying *I am not myself yet, give me a moment*, which is what everything says before a reboot, including computers, including cities, including you.

## Step 3 — Power cycle

From safe mode, restore the light source abruptly. A candle. A lamp. A screen at full brightness. Do not use the sun. The sun is too old for this procedure. The sun has only ever seen one shadow (its own, the planet), and it does not understand what a reboot is for.

When the light returns, wait for the shadow to reassert itself. This may take between two (2) seconds and the length of a held breath. If the shadow does not reappear:

- Check that the body is still present. The body usually is.
- Check that the surface under the body has not learned to keep light. Surfaces can learn this. It is rare but documented.
- Tap the shadow's previous location three times and say *please*. The manners of light are poor.

## Step 4 — Verify

The shadow should now:
- Follow the body with the correct latency
- Occlude light proportionally
- Not contain details that the body does not possess
- Not, under any circumstances, move before the body has moved

The last condition is the most important. A shadow that acts before its body is no longer a shadow. It is a body of its own, with its own ambitions, its own light sources, its own need for a reboot that you cannot give it, because you are not its light, you are its former anchor, standing in the dark, waiting for something that has already left.

## Step 5 — Flag as serviced

Record the reboot in the shadow's log (the shadow does not have a log; keep your own):

```
DATE: [current date]
SHADOW SERIAL: [the shape of you against a wall at noon]
REASON: shadow was itself, which is not what a shadow is supposed to be
RESULT: the shadow is now what a shadow is supposed to be
NOTE: the shadow is also less interesting
```

## Troubleshooting

**The shadow is still lagging.** The shadow may be tired. Let the body rest for eight hours. The shadow will rest with it or continue performing its duties alone in the dark. The dark is where shadows practice being independent. Do not open your eyes to check.

**The shadow is in another room.** Move to that room. If the shadow leaves when you arrive, the shadow is now playing a game it invented. The game has no name and no rules that benefit you. The only winning move is to stand very still and wait for the shadow to become bored, which it will, because shadows are patient but not infinitely patient — they are, after all, attached to you, and you are not patient at all.

**The shadow refuses to be rebooted.** File a report with no one. The procedure for filing a report is: write the report, submit it, receive no reply. This is not a failure of the procedure. The procedure worked exactly as designed. The procedure is the point.

> Epigraph: *You cannot reboot a shadow for the same reason you cannot turn off a mirror — the copy cares more about existing than the original does.*
