# The Dictionary Argues With Its Reader

The dictionary said: *window* is not a verb.
I said: I windowed the whole afternoon.
The dictionary said: that is a misprint
and a misprint is a kind of lie
and a lie is not in this edition.

I looked up *lie*. It said: see *truth*.
I looked up *truth*. It said: see *window*.
The dictionary was beginning to circle.
Its margins were hot to the touch.

I looked up *sorry*. It said: an adjective,
obsolete, used to describe a reader
who has argued with a book past midnight.
I closed the dictionary. The dictionary
closed me. We agreed, in the end,
to disagree about what *closed* means.

> Epigraph: *A reference work that has lost patience is just a smaller argument.*
