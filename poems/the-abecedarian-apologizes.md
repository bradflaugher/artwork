# The Abecedarian Apologizes

A is for the argument I started with the alphabet.
B is for the blame, which I have alphabetized.
C is for the comma I omitted, and the small crash that followed.
D is for the dog I described as *the dog*, which the dog resented.
E is for the evening I wasted looking up *evening*.
F is for the footnote that outgrew the foot.
G is for the grief, filed under *g*, which is also for *gone*.
H is for the hallway, which I have already apologized for elsewhere.
I is for the *I*, which has been overused in this confession.
J is for the joke, which was a hinge, and the hinge was rusted.
K is for the kettle, which I accused of being patient.
L is for the lie I told the lamp about the dark.
M is for the morning, which I misspelled as *mourning* and meant it.
N is for the no I delivered as a *maybe*, like a coward, in writing.
O is for the ode, which I owe to a clause and have not paid.
P is for the pocket, inventoried elsewhere, which is also for *please*.
Q is for the question I asked the dictionary and the dictionary asked back.
R is for the rain, which I reversed, and which has not forgiven me.
S is for the sorry, which is the whole point, and also a sentence.
T is for the Tuesday, which the town took, and which I helped hide.
U is for the undercoat of every excuse I have worn.
V is for the verb, *to window*, which is not a verb, and I am sorry.
W is for the weather, which I addressed as though it had a desk.
X is for the ex-, who is also for *gone*, and also for *still here*.
Y is for the yes I should have given to the question I dodged at N.
Z is for the zero, which is the number of poems that will fix any of this.

> Epigraph: *An apology that goes in order is just a confession with a filing system.*
