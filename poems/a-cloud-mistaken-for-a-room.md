# A Cloud Mistaken for a Room

We rented a cloud by accident.
The listing said *one room, north light,*
and we did not read the part
where the floor was a rumor
and the walls agreed to nothing.

At night the cloud changed its mind
about being a cloud.
It tried being a corridor,
then a held breath,
then the inside of someone
who had not arrived yet.

We hung our coats on the humidity.
We set the table for the weather.
A guest came and sat on the suggestion
of a chair and did not fall,
which is the closest thing to faith
we witnessed that whole year.

In the morning the cloud had moved
without us, taking the room
but leaving the address,
so that mail still comes
addressed to a place
that is now over the sea,
raining on no one,
keeping our forwarding silence
the way a cloud keeps everything:
briefly, and then as someone else's.

> Gloss: *A cloud is a room that has resigned from architecture.*
