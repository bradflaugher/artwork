# The Stars Keep a Separate Calendar

The stars are always answering
a question we have already stopped asking.
Their light arrives like a letter
forwarded through too many old addresses,
postmarked from a self we have since evicted.

We named them after animals
to make them keep still. It did not work.
They are still the animals; we are the constellations now,
strung between events we did not choose to connect,
a line drawn through our deaths and called a shape.

One star burned out before the cathedral was built
and its light has only just sat down in the pew.
It does not know the service is for it.
It does not know there was a cathedral.
It is here for a wedding that happened to no one,
on a Tuesday the sky has refused to surrender.

At night we lie on our backs and receive them,
all that punctual, exhausted brightness,
each point a fact about a place
that has, by now, almost certainly
changed its mind.

> Gloss: *A star is a rumor the dark keeps repeating long after the source has retracted it.*
