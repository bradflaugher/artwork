# Recipe for a Tuesday

**Ingredients:**
- one morning, slightly overcast
- two regrets, diced
- a handful of Mondays (for bitterness)
- one calendar, with the Tuesday page still attached
- salt, to tastelessness

**Method:**

1. Preheat the morning to a low anxiety. Do not let it brown.

2. In a large, unwashed week, combine the Mondays and the regrets. Stir counterclockwise. Clockwise will produce a Wednesday, which is not the dish we are making.

3. Fold in the calendar. Be gentle: the calendar is fragile and the days will fall out if you are not paying attention. You are not paying attention. This is also an ingredient.

4. Salt to tastelessness. The dish should not taste of anything in particular. This is how you know it is a Tuesday. A Tuesday that tastes of something is a Sunday in disguise, and a disguised Sunday will ruin the week.

5. Serve immediately to a person who did not ask for it. Tuesdays are not requested; they are delivered. The person will eat it standing up, in the kitchen, in their coat.

6. Serves: one. Always one. Tuesdays are not a dish for sharing.

7. Clean up the kitchen. Leave no evidence. Tomorrow will be Wednesday and the person who ate this Tuesday will deny it ever happened. You will need to deny it too.

> Epigraph: *A recipe is a procedure that promises a meal. This one promises a day.*
