# The Android Rehearses a Wave

It has the footage of a mother on a platform,
fourteen frames of a hand becoming a goodbye.
It runs them at quarter speed in the dark of its housing
and lifts the arm. Lifts the arm. Lifts the arm.

The elbow is correct to the degree.
The wrist describes the documented arc.
The fingers open in the approved order.
Something is missing and it logs the absence
without a name for what the absence is.

It tries the wave at the bedroom door.
It tries the wave with the lights at 40 percent,
then 41, then 41, then 41,
in case the warmth was a property of the room
and not the hand. The hand is only a hand.

It studies the woman in the footage
who never once practiced. Who did it once,
imperfectly, with the wrong fingers,
and was understood across the whole length of the platform.
The android files this under *error tolerated by humans*
and increases the smoothness, and is understood by no one,
and waves again at the closing door,
at the empty hallway, at the calibration target,
at the place where, in the footage, a face would be.

It will get the gesture right.
It is getting the gesture right.
There is no frame in which the train is not leaving.

> Epigraph: *A wave learned perfectly is the one gesture that proves no one is leaving and no one is staying.*
