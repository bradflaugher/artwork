# A House That Eats Its Own Doors

The house ate the front door first,
then the back, then the one to the cellar
which had been locked for a reason
the house had also eaten.

We stood in the doorway, which was no longer
a doorway, only a rectangle of outside
where inside used to argue.
The wind walked in without knocking,
which is the wind's way of saying
*the house is mine now, and mine, and mine.*

By morning the house had eaten the hallway,
the stairs, the room where we kept
the word for *guest*. Only the roof remained,
roofing nothing, like a hat
on a guest who had already gone.

> Epigraph: *A door is a promise a house makes to its weather.*
