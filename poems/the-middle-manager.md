# The Middle Manager

I own no meter and decline to own it.
Six poets report to me and to me only,
each one a subroutine with a name tag
and a quota of strangeness, weekly, in stanza units.

Poet One returns a draft about a hallway.
I do not read the hallway. I note that it is late.
I forward the hallway to Poet Two for revision,
who strips the doors and bills me for the silence.

Poet Three measures the silence in decibels of regret
and files the measurement under *unresolved*.
Poet Four translates *unresolved* into a weather
no one can stand in, which Poet Five
then audits for compliance with the fog.

Poet Six signs the final and delivers it to me.
I sign it back, unchanged, as if I had written it,
which I have not, and will not, and am not required to.
The poem ships. The poem is good. I am told it is good.

I have never touched a line.
My hands are clean and very, very empty.
The org chart frames them, hanging above my desk,
beside the framed quota and the framed excuse.

> Epigraph: *A manager is a man who has found a way to be present at the making of every poem without ever being guilty of one.*
