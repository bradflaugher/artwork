# Minutes of a Meeting That Never Adjourned

**Present:** the chair, the secretary, the ghost of an agenda, two abstentions, a draft.

**1. The meeting was called to order at an unspecified hour.**
The chair asked if all present were present. The secretary noted that *present* is a tense as well as a state, and that the meeting was therefore in two tenses at once. The chair ruled this out of order. The secretary appealed. The appeal is still pending.

**2. Old business.**
There is only old business. The meeting has no new business because the meeting has not ended, and new business requires an ending to be carried over from. The secretary read the minutes of the previous meeting, which were identical to these minutes, and were read at the previous meeting, which was also this meeting.

**3. Motion to adjourn.**
A motion to adjourn was made. The motion was seconded by the draft. The chair asked whether a draft may second a motion. The secretary consulted the bylaws. The bylaws were found to be a previous draft of these minutes. The motion to adjourn was tabled until the next meeting, which is also this meeting.

**4. New business.**
See item 2.

**5. The meeting did not adjourn.**
The chair left. The secretary left. The agenda remained, taking attendance, in a room whose lights turn off only when the room is empty, and which has therefore never been dark.

> Epigraph: *A meeting that cannot end is not a meeting. It is a room with opinions.*
