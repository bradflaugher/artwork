# The Cloud Files a Complaint

The cloud arrived at the window
with a clipboard made of weather
and asked to see the lease.

We said the sky was public.
The cloud wrote that down
as our first admission.

All morning it inspected the roof
for evidence of upward living:
chimney smoke, antenna prayers,
the blue bowl of a satellite dish
collecting signals meant for rain.

By noon it had cited us
for harboring ceilings.
By three it had darkened
into the shape of a signature
no one wanted to forge.

When it finally opened,
it did not rain.
It released copies of itself,
thin gray pages sticking to our faces,
each one stamped insufficient altitude.

We appealed by looking up.
The cloud denied ever having a body.
This is how weather wins:
it becomes paperwork
only after soaking through the file.

> Gloss: *A cloud is a witness that changes shape before the oath is finished.*
