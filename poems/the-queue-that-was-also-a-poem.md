# The Queue That Was Also a Poem

The queue began at the bus stop
and by the second Tuesday
it had acquired a refrain:

*please move down,*
*please move down,*
*there is plenty of standing
behind the standing.*

By the third Tuesday the queue
had a stanza break where the old woman
in the green coat had died,
and everyone stepped over the gap
politely, the way one steps
over a line break, without
mentioning it.

The queue rhymed, sort of:
the man with the cane
and the man with the cane
and the man with the cane,
three beats, then a rest,
then the bus, which was also a refrain
because it never came.

Someone tried to leave the queue
and the queue did not notice.
This is how you know the queue
is a poem and not a line:
a line notices when you leave it;
a poem keeps going
in the voice of whoever is still there,
which is everyone,
which is no one,
which is the same thing
in a queue that has been standing
since the bus stop learned its own name.

*please move down,*
*please move down,*
*there is plenty of poem
behind the poem.*

> Epigraph: *A queue is a line break that waits. A poem is a queue that rhymes with its own leaving.*
